This is the first step you will follow when attempting to cross stitch. I've read that this step is to read a cross stitch chart. This tells you where stitches will go and what colors they will be. The roadmap to success you could say.

Usually Aida or Linen. Make sure to bind the endges so they don't fray. Masking tape or a zig zag stitch can reduce fraying.

Fold in half one way, then folld in half the other way. The intersection of these folds will be your center. Mark that with either a small stitch or a pin.

This is optional of course and there are plenty of hoop and frame options. Beginning stitchers may find a hoop easy to set fabric in.

Find your beginning color and cut an 18 inch span. Any longer and you could end up fighting knots. Usually embroidery floss is made of 6 strands of thread, you want to only use two of these threads to stitch with at a time. Seperate each strand and group in twos.
